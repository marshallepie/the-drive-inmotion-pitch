Executive framing

The Drive is not being positioned as a speculative coin. The token launch is the financing and network-formation layer for a broader marketplace, escrow, inventory, trade, and vehicle-backed finance platform.

The capital plan is staged so valuation expansion tracks infrastructure readiness, partner onboarding, legal preparation, utility deployment, and public-market maturity rather than pure hype.

Roadmap

One roadmap for platform execution and token execution.

01

Phase 1 — Entity, compliance, and launch architecture

Lock token structure, legal opinions, corporate entities, jurisdiction strategy, disclosure pack, treasury policy, vesting rules, and core investor materials. Friends & Family funds this preparation layer.

02

Phase 2 — Utility integration into the marketplace stack

Connect DRIVE to marketplace mechanics: fees, access layers, partner rewards, inventory participation, and finance-linked use cases tied to the real platform roadmap.

03

Phase 3 — Strategic distribution and corridor expansion

Use Private Round 2 capital to deepen PEKEMA / Malaysia corridor execution, onboard strategic partners, and extend vehicle-backed finance, escrow, and trade infrastructure.

04

Phase 4 — Pre-public readiness

Finalize market-making, exchange-readiness, token documentation, launch calendar, KOL / distribution coordination, and public investor communications.

05

Phase 5 — Public / TGE launch

Launch into the market with clearer utility, stronger disclosures, better partner proof, and a more disciplined route to the £1B public target.

Positioning discipline

The token story has to read as serious infrastructure finance, not meme finance.

What to emphasise

  • Real economic system around vehicles, trade, escrow, and finance
  • Structured valuation progression rather than arbitrary pump language
  • Cross-border automotive and RWA logic with real corridor strategy
  • Compliance readiness and sequencing discipline

What to avoid

  • Overclaiming regulation, exchange listings, or guaranteed returns
  • Mixing venture seed language with public-token language on the same page
  • Presenting the token as disconnected from platform utility
  • Using retail-hype language where institutional tone is needed
